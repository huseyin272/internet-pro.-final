Gilindire Cave

About

Gilindire Cave, also known as Astım Cave, is a natural wonder located near the town of Aydıncık in Mersin province, Turkey. It is renowned for its unique geological formations, underground rivers, and archaeological discoveries.

Discovery

Gilindire Cave was discovered in 1999 during quarrying activities in the area. Since then, it has been the subject of extensive scientific research and exploration, uncovering evidence of ancient human occupation and prehistoric animal remains.

Geology

The cave features impressive stalactites, stalagmites, and columns formed over millions of years by the dissolution of limestone rocks. Its underground rivers and chambers add to its allure, attracting speleologists and adventurers from around the world.

Visit

Gilindire Cave is open to visitors, offering guided tours led by experienced guides. Visitors can explore its caverns, admire its geological formations, and learn about its history and significance. The cave's cool temperatures make it a refreshing escape from the summer heat.
