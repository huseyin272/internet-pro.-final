Bodrum Castle and Underwater Archaeology Museum

About

Bodrum Castle, also known as the Castle of St. Peter, is a historical landmark located in Bodrum, Turkey. It was built by the Knights of St. John in the 15th century and has served as a fortress, prison, and museum throughout its history.

History

The castle has a rich and diverse history, reflecting the various civilizations that have ruled the region over the centuries. It has witnessed numerous battles and undergone several renovations and expansions to become the imposing structure that stands today.

Exhibits

The Bodrum Underwater Archaeology Museum, housed within the castle's walls, showcases artifacts recovered from shipwrecks in the Aegean and Mediterranean seas. The exhibits provide insight into ancient maritime trade, navigation, and seafaring cultures.

Visit

The castle and museum are open to visitors who can explore its medieval architecture, panoramic views of the Aegean Sea, and fascinating exhibits. Guided tours are available to learn more about the castle's history and the artifacts on display.
