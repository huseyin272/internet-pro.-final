Ayasofya Mosque

About

Ayasofya Mosque, also known as Hagia Sophia, is a historic architectural marvel located in Istanbul, Turkey. Originally built as a Christian cathedral in the 6th century during the Byzantine Empire, it was later converted into a mosque in the 15th century during the Ottoman Empire.

History

The history of Ayasofya spans over 1,500 years, witnessing various political and religious transformations. It served as the principal church of the Byzantine Empire, then as the principal mosque of the Ottoman Empire, and later as a museum in the modern era.

Architecture

Ayasofya is renowned for its majestic dome, innovative architectural features, and intricate mosaics. It blends elements of Byzantine and Islamic architecture, reflecting the cultural and religious fusion that characterizes Istanbul.

Visit

Ayasofya Mosque is open to visitors throughout the week, except for certain prayer times. Visitors can marvel at its grandeur, explore its history through guided tours, and admire its stunning architecture and religious significance.
