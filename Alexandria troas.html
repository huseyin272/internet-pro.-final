Alexandria Troas Archaeological Site

About

Alexandria Troas is an ancient city located in modern-day Turkey, near the village of Dalyan. Founded by the Macedonian king Alexander the Great in the 4th century BC, it served as an important port city and hub of commerce and culture in the region.

History

Alexandria Troas flourished under various rulers, including the Romans and Byzantines, who further developed the city's infrastructure and architectural landmarks. It was a significant center for trade, religion, and philosophy, attracting visitors from across the Mediterranean.

Excavations

Archaeological excavations at Alexandria Troas have uncovered a wealth of artifacts and structures, including a theater, baths, temples, and a defensive wall. These excavations provide valuable insights into the city's urban layout, daily life, and cultural interactions.

Visit

Today, visitors to Alexandria Troas can explore the archaeological site and marvel at its ancient ruins and monuments. Guided tours are available to learn about the city's history, archaeology, and significance in the ancient world.
