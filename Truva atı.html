About

The Trojan Horse, also known as Truva At覺 in Turkish, is a legendary wooden horse that played a central role in the Trojan War, as described in Greek mythology. It is said to have been used by the Greeks to infiltrate the city of Troy and achieve victory.

History

The story of the Trojan Horse is recounted in Homer's epic poem, the Iliad. According to the legend, after a prolonged siege of Troy, the Greeks pretended to sail away, leaving behind the massive wooden horse as a gift to the Trojans.

Construction

The construction of the Trojan Horse is shrouded in myth and legend. According to some accounts, it was built by the Greek artisan Epeius, while others attribute its construction to the divine craftsman Daedalus.

Visit

While the original Trojan Horse was a mythical creation, replicas and artistic interpretations can be found in various locations around the world, including museums and historical sites. These replicas serve as reminders of the enduring legacy of the Trojan War and its impact on Western culture.
