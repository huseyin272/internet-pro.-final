Kaymaklı Underground City (Kaymaklı Yeraltı Şehri)

About

Kaymaklı Underground City is an ancient subterranean settlement located in the Cappadocia region of Turkey. It is one of several underground cities in the area and is renowned for its vast network of tunnels, chambers, and living spaces carved into the soft volcanic rock.

History

The origins of Kaymaklı Underground City date back to the Hittite period in the 8th century BC, although it was later expanded and used by various civilizations, including the early Christians and Byzantines, as a refuge during times of war and persecution.

Architecture

The underground city consists of multiple levels, with interconnected tunnels and rooms carved out of the rock. It features ventilation shafts, wells, churches, storage areas, and living quarters, providing insights into ancient underground living conditions.

Visit

Today, visitors to Kaymaklı Underground City can explore its labyrinthine passages and marvel at its architectural ingenuity. Guided tours are available to learn about the city's history, architecture, and cultural significance, offering a unique glimpse into Turkey's ancient past.
