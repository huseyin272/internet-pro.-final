Pamukkale

About

Pamukkale, meaning "cotton castle" in Turkish, is a natural wonder located in the Denizli Province of southwestern Turkey. It is renowned for its surreal landscape of terraced thermal pools and white travertine formations, formed over thousands of years by mineral-rich thermal waters.

Geology

The terraces of Pamukkale are made of travertine, a type of sedimentary rock deposited by mineral springs. The unique geological formations were created as calcium carbonate dissolved in the hot spring water precipitated and solidified over time, forming the cascading terraces seen today.

Thermal Pools

Pamukkale's thermal pools are renowned for their mineral-rich waters, which are believed to have therapeutic properties. Visitors can soak in the warm, mineral-rich pools while enjoying panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, making it a popular destination for relaxation and wellness.

Visit

Pamukkale is open to visitors year-round, with the best time to visit being in the spring or autumn when the weather is mild. Visitors can explore the terraces, walk barefoot in the thermal pools, and visit nearby ancient ruins, including the Hierapolis archaeological site.
