Amyntas Rock Tombs

About

The Amyntas Rock Tombs are ancient burial chambers carved into the cliffsides of Fethiye, Turkey. Dating back to the 4th century BC, these tombs are remarkable examples of Lycian funerary architecture and are named after the Lycian ruler Amyntas.

History

The tombs were carved during the Hellenistic period and were used as burial sites for wealthy and prominent individuals of the time. They feature intricate facades adorned with reliefs, including images of gods, mythical creatures, and scenes from daily life.

Architecture

The tombs exhibit a variety of architectural styles, reflecting the cultural influences of the Lycians, Greeks, and Persians. They are typically composed of a main chamber with a facade resembling a temple or house, accessed via a rock-cut staircase.

Visit

Today, visitors to Fethiye can explore the Amyntas Rock Tombs and marvel at their impressive craftsmanship and historical significance. The site offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular attraction for tourists and history enthusiasts.
