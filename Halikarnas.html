Halicarnassus Mausoleum

About

The Halicarnassus Mausoleum, also known simply as the Mausoleum, was a monumental tomb built in Halicarnassus (modern-day Bodrum, Turkey) for Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire, and his wife Artemisia II of Caria.

History

Constructed between 353 and 350 BC, the Mausoleum was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It stood approximately 45 meters (148 feet) in height and was adorned with intricate sculptures and reliefs.

Architecture

The Mausoleum was designed by the Greek architects Satyros and Pythius. It consisted of a rectangular base, a tiered podium, a colonnade of Ionic columns, and a pyramid-shaped roof adorned with sculptures depicting mythological scenes and figures.

Visit

Today, only the foundations and some fragments of the Mausoleum remain. Visitors to Bodrum can explore the site of the Mausoleum, which is now an open-air museum, and learn about its history, architecture, and significance in antiquity.
